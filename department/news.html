Memorandum of Understanding Between AIU and EPAM Kyrgyzstan

Published: October 2024 | Category: Partnerships & Collaborations
Strategic Partnership Industry Collaboration Educational Development
Signing ceremony of the memorandum of understanding between AIU and EPAM Kyrgyzstan

In a significant step towards strengthening industry-academia collaboration, Ala-Too International University and EPAM Kyrgyzstan formalized their partnership through a memorandum of understanding signed in October 2024.

"This partnership represents our commitment to bridging the gap between academic learning and industry requirements, creating meaningful opportunities for our students to thrive in the global technology landscape."

Within the framework of this cooperation, EPAM Kyrgyzstan will actively contribute to enhancing educational programs for computer science students. The collaboration focuses on curriculum development, practical training, and creating new pathways for professional growth and career advancement.

A key initiative of this partnership involves EPAM Kyrgyzstan selecting promising candidates to participate in an internal laboratory program. This immersive experience will allow students to develop their technical skills while working on real-world projects under the guidance of industry experts.

EPAM Kyrgyzstan operates as part of the global EPAM Systems, Inc., a leading digital transformation services and product engineering company with presence in 50 countries worldwide. The company specializes in digital product development, cloud transformation, data analytics, quality engineering, and comprehensive technology solutions for clients across various industries.

Ala-Too International University extends its sincere appreciation to EPAM Kyrgyzstan for their warm reception and enthusiastic collaboration. The university anticipates a fruitful and long-lasting partnership that will significantly benefit students, faculty, and the broader technology ecosystem in Kyrgyzstan.

Source: EPAM Kyrgyzstan Official Communications | For more information: Contact the Department of Computer Science
