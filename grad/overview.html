Joint Master's Program in Computer Science

The Kyrgyz-German Faculty of Applied Informatics announces admission to the joint Master's program in cooperation with KSUCTA, Ala-Too International University, and West Saxon University of Applied Sciences Zwickau in the following specializations:

1. International Software Systems Design

This specialization is based on the Master's program in Computer Science at West Saxon University of Applied Sciences Zwickau, with an emphasis on software development. The provided content is also adapted to the needs of local enterprises that work in large-scale software development and have high demand for software developers at the Master's level. During the curriculum development, discussions and negotiations were conducted with managers in development and management of numerous companies and organizations. Language training and intercultural education enable successful work in international teams. Instruction is conducted in English, German, and Russian.

2. Information Technology Entrepreneurship

This specialization is business-oriented, aiming to provide students with software products for commercial exploitation and related organizational structures, as well as necessary management skills. The curriculum content facilitates the transfer of knowledge and technology to the Kyrgyz economy and strengthens the existing IT industry both nationally and internationally. In this specialization, the main focus is on the technical foundations of software development, business components related to the exploitation of inventions, intercultural aspects, and language training. Practical modules in the 2nd and 3rd semesters will enable graduates to successfully create and implement new projects within existing enterprises. Instruction is conducted in English, German, and Russian.

Standard Duration of Study:

Master's program - 3 years.
